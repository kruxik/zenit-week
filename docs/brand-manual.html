01
Introduction

Zenit Week is a visual week planner built on the Jar of Life principle — protect your big rocks first, then let the rest fill in. The brand reflects this philosophy: intentional, calm, and action-oriented. Not a task manager. Not a calendar. A tool for people who plan with purpose.


The logotype encodes three meanings in one word: Zenith (where you want to be), Zen (the mental clarity you achieve by planning well), and it (the act of doing). The mind-map symbol between them is not decoration — it is the product, rendered as a letterform.

Brand values
Intentional

Every element earns its place. Nothing decorative.

Calm

Zen is the outcome. The visual language should feel it.

Structural

Radial, organised, clear. Like the mind map itself.

02
Logo Anatomy

The logotype has four components. Each is load-bearing — none may be removed, reordered, or substituted.

Component descriptions
① "Zen" — Light text

Weight 300. Sets the meditative, intentional tone. The contrast with "it" (bold) is the visual pun made tangible.

② Brandmark — Mind-map symbol

Root node (parent) connected via bezier curves to two child nodes. Upper child in brand purple, lower in brand blue. This is the product in symbol form.

③ "it" — Bold text

Weight 700. Signals action — doing, completing, getting things done. The weight contrast with "Zen" encodes the brand tension: calm meets action.

④ "Week" — Light text

Weight 300. Anchors the time horizon. Makes the product category immediately clear — this is a weekly planning tool.

Symbol anatomy
Root node — Parent. Connects "Zen" to the branching structure. Rendered in the primary text colour (dark or light depending on background).
Child A (purple) — Upper branch. Aligned to cap-height of adjacent letters. Always #A259FF — never recoloured.
Child B (blue) — Lower branch. Aligned to baseline of adjacent letters. Always #1ABCFE — never recoloured.
Bezier curves — Cubic, forking from the same point on the root's right edge. Mirror the exact curve style used in the Zenit Week mind-map interface.
03
Logo Variations

Three approved forms. Use the fullest form the context allows.

Full logotype — Primary

Use this form whenever space permits. Preferred for app toolbar, marketing, and all primary contexts.

Compact logotype — Secondary

"Zenit" without "Week". Use in constrained horizontal spaces where the time horizon is already contextually clear.

Brandmark — Icon only

Standalone symbol for favicon, app icon, avatar, or any square/constrained context. Never use the brandmark in place of the full logotype where space allows.

The logotype uses the system UI font stack — no custom typeface is required or embedded. This ensures the logo renders natively on every platform: SF Pro on macOS/iOS, Segoe UI on Windows, Roboto on Android.

The weight contrast rule

The contrast between weight 300 ("Zen", "Week") and weight 700 ("it") is a core brand element — it makes the Zen/it pun visible typographically. This contrast must be preserved. Do not use a single weight throughout the logotype.

08
Misuse

These examples show what must never be done with the Zenit Week logo.

Do not
✕
Do not stretch or distort proportions
✕
Do not alter the font weight contrast (Zen must be light, it must be bold)
✕
Do not recolour the brandmark nodes or branches
✕
Do not remove the brandmark symbol from the logotype
✕
Do not rotate or tilt the logo
✕
Do not add shadows, glows, outlines, or visual effects
09
File Formats & Usage

The Zenit Week logo is defined in SVG. Because it uses only system fonts and geometric primitives, it requires no external assets and renders identically across all modern browsers and platforms.
